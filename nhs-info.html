NHS Info

Understanding your NHS dental entitlements.

NHS dental charges

NHS dental charges are set by the government and apply to all NHS dental practices. There are three standard charge bands.

Band What's included Cost
Band 1 Examination, diagnosis, X-rays, scale and polish, prevention advice £26.80
Band 2 Everything in Band 1 plus fillings, root canals, extractions £73.50
Band 3 Everything in Bands 1 & 2 plus crowns, dentures, bridges £319.10

Who gets free NHS dental treatment?

You're entitled to free NHS dental care if you:

  • Are under 18 (or under 19 and in full-time education)
  • Are pregnant or have had a baby in the last 12 months
  • Are being treated in an NHS hospital
  • Receive Income Support, Income-based JSA, or Universal Credit
  • Hold a valid HC2 certificate

NHS vs private treatment

NHS dentistry covers clinically necessary treatment. For cosmetic options like whitening, veneers, Invisalign, or facial aesthetics, private treatment is available. Our team will always explain your options clearly so you can make an informed decision.
